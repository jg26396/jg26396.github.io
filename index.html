We are a group of Electrical and Computer Engineering undergraduate senior students from Georgia Southern University. As a final requirement of our college career, we must conduct and develop a research project that encapsulates and puts to practice the knowledge we have gained so far. This webpage is dedicated to documenting the development of our senior design project.

About us

Hello everyone!

For our senior design project, we decided to develop an autonomous fire detection unmanned aerial vehicle (UAV / Drone). We structured our project into three different areas: mechanical design, electrical design, and software development. We have created our mechanical design from scratch and focused on several variables (thrust, payload, aerodynamics, etc.) to successfully build a quadcopter UAV. The electrical design relied upon the proper application circuit analysis and control systems methods to successfully power and control the UAV. Finally, the last area, Software Development, consisted of the implementation of knowledge on Microcontrollers to command the electrical components such as motors and sensors. We implemented Convolutional Neural Networks to successfully identify fires in real-time with the Raspberry Pi and the Pi Camera Module. This would allow the drone to leverage computer vision algorithms to find fire-affected areas faster. Additionally, we also implemented simulation software (ArduPilot / DroneKit) for drone movement by using Python commands to the Pix Hawk controller.

We decided to develop this project due to the possible effects that it can have in life-threatening situations, in this case, fire. The implementation of an autonomous drone that leverages computer vision algorithms to help address a fire could save lives. Firefighters would be able to address the human victims faster while a drone takes charge of putting off the fire. The firefighters would also be less exposed to smoke inhalation and burns. We believe that this project is able to improve the front-line response to fire hazards.

Design of drone

First design

The first iteration was a 3 tiered canopy that only allowed space for one microcontroller. We decided to step away from this design as there was no space for the likes of a camera and a place to store the battery.

Second design

The second design included making the canopy one solid piece instead of having plates stacked on top of each other. During test, this design proved to not be level and vibration resistant during flight.

Final design

The final design went back to follow the tiered structure which would allow for the most surface area to mount components while also keeping the frame relatively stable and lightweight.
